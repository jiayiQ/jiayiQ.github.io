Data Collection: Data collection is a crucial initial step in meaningful quant trading. It's essential to gather precise and accurate data because without it, even the most sophisticated strategies in the black box become useless. As the saying goes, "garbage in, garbage out." Quantitative strategies heavily rely on various data sources, including price data, trading volume, open market interest rates, and other metrics related to trading activity. However, data availability and quality can pose challenges, particularly when it comes to capturing infrequent or irregular events.

Strategy Design: A good trading strategy often involves complexity and the utilization of various models to create a well-rounded portfolio. For example, an alpha model aims to generate profits by capturing signals, a risk model acts as a protective guardian to monitor and minimize risks, and transaction cost models are employed, among others.

Systematic Execution: When it comes to NLP models like ChatGPT 3 or 4, their remarkable ability to understand human natural language becomes incredibly valuable. In quant trading, it becomes more critical to comprehend the data being used rather than focusing solely on understanding the strategy itself. Fundamental data, which includes factors like financial health, performance, worth, and sentiment, plays a crucial role in strategy selection.

Market Dynamics: Predicting the future, especially with accuracy, is a challenging task in quant trading. Traditional quant trading approaches rely on technical sentiment strategies that track indicators such as trading prices, volume, and volatility to assess expected returns. Interestingly, recent research has indicated that sentiment analysis scores derived from ChatGPT actually outperform traditional sentiment scores.
